Considering or managing a family office?

If you have reached a point where managing your personal wealth (separate from the family business) is becoming more than a full time job, you may want to consider a family office. You may consider sharing costs by becoming part of a multi-family office (MFO), or set up your single family office (SFO).

Family offices help you grow (investments), protect (wealth planning) and manage (administration) your wealth. They can also help make more money by helping to grow or manage the family business. Hence, family offices oversee -

  • Investments – including traditional, alternative and strategic investments
  • Wealth Planning – including family governance, structuring, estate plan ning, tax planning, legacy/philanthropy

Looking for service providers?

There are numerous providers in the market offering services related to legal, tax, accounting, technology, governance consulting, trust services etc. But do they have the experience to handle your specific issue? Have they successfully handled other families’ issues? Do they come highly recommended?

You can not only look up service providers but also see ratings given by other member families.

Struggling with due diligence on alternative funds?

You probably get pitched to on a daily basis. Promoters of Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs) will tell you that their product is a good investment. Your private banker or client relationship manager also brings a number of ideas to you. But where do you get an independent assessment on these? Does your CFO or chartered accountant really have the time or expertise to assess private equity and real estate funds? How do you conduct proper due diligence on these alternative funds?

Want to discuss with your peers?

Private Investor Network

The Private Investor Network is a membership-based network for private investors and single family offices, primarily in India. We also welcome Indian families residing overseas or indeed any global families with interest in investing in India. Hence, you will be able to get insights from your peers, not only from remote parts of India but also the world.

The aim is to provide a ‘safe harbour’ for them to share experiences and learn from each other and from a curated list of rated experts We hope the content and other resources will spark the discussion, which will give you the opportunity to share and learn from your own peers.

PIN provides the following services –

  • Networking and peer sharing – through online discussion forums
  • Learning – through online library of articles, interviews, videos, podcasts etc
  • Directory of experts and service providers
  • Events for networking and learning
  • Exclusive partner services – such as due diligence portal, executive education, nextgen education etc
